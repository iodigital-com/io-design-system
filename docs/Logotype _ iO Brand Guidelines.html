Logotype

The logotype is the most important element of our identity - our universal signature across all communications. It’s created with focus on the contrast between hard and round edges.

Download logo

Logotype
Colours

Our logotype is mainly used in black or white. On simple and light backgrounds, such as the fifth element or any secondary colour, the primary blue colour can be used. You can use the secondary colours on the primary blue background.


White


Black


Accent colour

Logotype
Colour treatment


White / Negative
The most common colour treatment. Strive to use a white version.


Black / Positive
When the white logotype gives to low contrast, use the black one.


Accent colour
Only apply the logotype in Energetic Blue on calm background as our base colour or fifth element.

Logotype
Placements


To ensure that our logotype always stands out while being a natural part of the overall design, it can be placed in seven different places. If you feel uncertain where to place it, pick the bottom right or the top left corner.

Logotype
Clearspace


When using the logo in a design or placing it next to other visual elements, you should ensure that it has plenty of room to breathe. This is where clearspace comes into play. The logotypes clearspace defines the minimal distance between the logo and any graphic element it may be sitting next to in a composition. Use half of the height of the logo as a reference for the appropriate clearspace.

Minimal clearspace = Height logo x 0.5
For example: if you were to place the logotype, sized 30px tall, in a logo pool next to other logos, you should ensure that the io logo has at least 15px of clearspace on all sides.

Logotype
Alternative placement


You can enlarge the logo beyond its container. We only do this at the bottom right corner of an artboard.

Do's and don'ts


Do
Only use the logotype in a solid colour with high contrast.


Do
Always follow placement rules when you place the logotype.


Do
Only use alternative placements if the situation allows.


Don't
Never place the logotype with little tonal contrast between the background and logotype.


Don't
Do not rotate the logo on print (rotation is permitted in animations).


Don't
Do not change the colour of the logo to any other colour then the allowed ones.


Don't
Do not transform the logo out of proportion or scale the elements independently.


Don't
Do not use gradients.
